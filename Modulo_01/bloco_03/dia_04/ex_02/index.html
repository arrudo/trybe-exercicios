Informações gerais

Odontodactylus scyllarus, Mantis shrimp, or Stomatopods, are carnivorous marine crustaceans of the order Stomatopoda, branching from other members of the class Malacostraca around 340 million years ago.

Espécies

About 450 species of mantis shrimp have been discovered worldwide; all living species are in the suborder Unipeltata, which arose around 193 million years ago.

Habitat

Mantis shrimp live in burrows where they spend the majority of their time.[8] The two different categories of mantis shrimp—spearing and smashing—favour different locations for burrowing.[8] The spearing species build their habitat in soft sediments and the smashing species make burrows in hard substrata or coral cavities.
